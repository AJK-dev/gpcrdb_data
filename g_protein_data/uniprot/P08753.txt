CC   -!- FUNCTION: Heterotrimeric guanine nucleotide-binding proteins (G
CC       proteins) function as transducers downstream of G protein-coupled
CC       receptors (GPCRs) in numerous signaling cascades. The alpha chain
CC       contains the guanine nucleotide binding site and alternates
CC       between an active, GTP-bound state and an inactive, GDP-bound
CC       state. Signaling by an activated GPCR promotes GDP release and GTP
CC       binding. The alpha subunit has a low GTPase activity that converts
CC       bound GTP to GDP, thereby terminating the signal (PubMed:2159473).
CC       Both GDP release and GTP hydrolysis are modulated by numerous
CC       regulatory proteins. Signaling is mediated via effector proteins,
CC       such as adenylate cyclase. Inhibits adenylate cyclase activity,
CC       leading to decreased intracellular cAMP levels. Stimulates the
CC       activity of receptor-regulated K(+) channels. The active GTP-bound
CC       form prevents the association of RGS14 with centrosomes and is
CC       required for the translocation of RGS14 from the cytoplasm to the
CC       plasma membrane. May play a role in cell division.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08754, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2159473}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterotrimeric G proteins are composed of 3 units; alpha,
CC       beta and gamma. The alpha subunit contains the guanine nucleotide
CC       binding site (PubMed:2159473). GTP binding causes dissociation of
CC       the heterotrimer, liberating the individual subunits so that they
CC       can interact with downstream effector proteins. Interacts with
CC       GPSM1 (PubMed:11121039). Interacts (GDP-bound form) with GPSM2
CC       (via GoLoco domains). Does not interact with RGS2. Interacts with
CC       RGS8 and RGS10; this strongly enhances the intrinsic GTPase
CC       activity (By similarity). Interacts with RGS12 (PubMed:11387333).
CC       Interacts with RGS16; this strongly enhances the intrinsic GTPase
CC       activity (By similarity). Interacts (via active GTP- or inactive
CC       GDP-bound form) with RGS14 (PubMed:11387333, PubMed:16870394).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08754, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9DC51,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11121039, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11387333,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16870394, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2159473}.
