CC   -!- FUNCTION: Guanine nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins) are
CC       involved as modulators or transducers in various transmembrane
CC       signaling systems. G(olf) alpha mediates signal transduction
CC       within the olfactory neuroepithelium and the basal ganglia. May be
CC       involved in some aspect of visual transduction, and in mediating
CC       the effect of one or more hormones/neurotransmitters.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: G proteins are composed of 3 units; alpha, beta and
CC       gamma. The alpha chain contains the guanine nucleotide binding
CC       site.

CC   -!- DISEASE: Dystonia 25 (DYT25) [MIM:615073]: A form of dystonia, a
CC       disorder defined by the presence of sustained involuntary muscle
CC       contraction, often leading to abnormal postures. DYT25 is an
CC       autosomal dominant neurologic disorder characterized by adult
CC       onset of focal dystonia, usually involving the neck. The dystonia
CC       most often progresses to involve other regions, particularly the
CC       face and laryngeal muscles, and less commonly the trunk and limbs.
