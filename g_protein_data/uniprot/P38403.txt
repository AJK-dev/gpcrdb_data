CC   -!- FUNCTION: Heterotrimeric guanine nucleotide-binding proteins (G
CC       proteins) function as transducers downstream of G protein-coupled
CC       receptors (GPCRs) in numerous signaling cascades. The alpha chain
CC       contains the guanine nucleotide binding site and alternates
CC       between an active, GTP-bound state and an inactive, GDP-bound
CC       state. Signaling by an activated GPCR promotes GDP release and GTP
CC       binding. The alpha subunit has a low GTPase activity that converts
CC       bound GTP to GDP, thereby terminating the signal. Both GDP release
CC       and GTP hydrolysis are modulated by numerous regulatory proteins.
CC       Signaling is mediated via effector proteins, such as adenylate
CC       cyclase. Inhibits adenylate cyclase activity, leading to decreased
CC       intracellular cAMP levels. Stimulates the activity of receptor-
CC       regulated K(+) channels. The active GTP-bound form prevents the
CC       association of RGS14 with centrosomes and is required for the
CC       translocation of RGS14 from the cytoplasm to the plasma membrane.
CC       May play a role in cell division. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08754}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterotrimeric G proteins are composed of 3 units; alpha,
CC       beta and gamma. The alpha subunit contains the guanine nucleotide
CC       binding site. GTP binding causes dissociation of the heterotrimer,
CC       liberating the individual subunits so that they can interact with
CC       downstream effector proteins. Interacts with GPSM1 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts (GDP-bound form) with GPSM2 (via GoLoco
CC       domains). Does not interact with RGS2. Interacts with RGS8 and
CC       RGS10; this strongly enhances the intrinsic GTPase activity.
CC       Interacts with RGS12 (By similarity). Interacts with RGS16; this
CC       strongly enhances the intrinsic GTPase activity (By similarity).
CC       Interacts (via active GTP- or inactive GDP-bound form) with RGS14
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08753,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08754, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9DC51}.

CC       Cell membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08754}. Cytoplasm,
CC       cytoskeleton, microtubule organizing center, centrosome
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08754}. Membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08754}; Lipid-anchor
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08754}. Note=Localizes in the centrosomes
CC       of interphase and mitotic cells. Detected at the cleavage furrow
CC       and/or the midbody. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P08754}.
