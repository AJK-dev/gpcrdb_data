CC   -!- FUNCTION: Guanine nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins)
CC       function as transducers downstream of G protein-coupled receptors
CC       (GPCRs) in numerous signaling cascades. The alpha chain contains
CC       the guanine nucleotide binding site and alternates between an
CC       active, GTP-bound state and an inactive, GDP-bound state.
CC       Signaling by an activated GPCR promotes GDP release and GTP
CC       binding. The alpha subunit has a low GTPase activity that converts
CC       bound GTP to GDP, thereby terminating the signal. Both GDP release
CC       and GTP hydrolysis are modulated by numerous regulatory proteins
CC       (By similarity). Signaling is mediated via effector proteins, such
CC       as adenylate cyclase. Inhibits adenylate cyclase activity, leading
CC       to decreased intracellular cAMP levels (By similarity). The
CC       inactive GDP-bound form prevents the association of RGS14 with
CC       centrosomes and is required for the translocation of RGS14 from
CC       the cytoplasm to the plasma membrane. Required for normal
CC       cytokinesis during mitosis (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10824, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63096}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterotrimeric G proteins are composed of 3 units; alpha,
CC       beta and gamma. The alpha chain contains the guanine nucleotide
CC       binding site. Identified in complex with the beta subunit GNB1 and
CC       the gamma subunit GNG1. Identified in complex with the beta
CC       subunit GNB1 and the gamma subunit GNG2. GTP binding causes
CC       dissociation of the heterotrimer, liberating the individual
CC       subunits so that they can interact with downstream effector
CC       proteins (By similarity). Interacts (GDP-bound form) with GPSM1;
CC       this inhibits guanine nucleotide exchange and GTP binding (By
CC       similarity). Interacts (GDP-bound form) with GPSM2 (via GoLoco
CC       domains); this inhibits guanine nucleotide exchange. Interacts
CC       with RGS10; this strongly enhances GTP hydrolysis. Interacts with
CC       RGS1 and RGS16; this strongly enhances GTPase activity. Interacts
CC       with RGS4. Interacts with RGS12. Interacts (via active GTP- or
CC       inactive GDP-bound forms) with RGS14 (via RGS and GoLoco domains).
CC       Interacts with RGS3, RGS6, RGS7, RGS8, RGS17, RGS18 and RGS20 (in
CC       vitro) (By similarity). Interacts (GDP-bound form) with RIC8A (via
CC       C-terminus) (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10824,
CC       ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63096}.

CC       Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10824}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10824}; Peripheral membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10824}; Cytoplasmic side
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10824}. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton,
CC       microtubule organizing center, centrosome
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10824}. Membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63096}; Lipid-anchor
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63096}. Note=Localizes in the centrosomes
CC       of interphase and mitotic cells, but not in centrosomes during
CC       cytokinesis. Detected at the cleavage furrow or the midbody (By
CC       similarity). Localized at the plasma membrane throughout mitosis.
CC       Colocalizes with RIC8A and RGS14 at the plasma membrane (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10824}.
