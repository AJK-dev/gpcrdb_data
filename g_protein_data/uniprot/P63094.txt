CC   -!- FUNCTION: Guanine nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins)
CC       function as transducers in numerous signaling pathways controlled
CC       by G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs). Signaling involves the
CC       activation of adenylyl cyclases, resulting in increased levels of
CC       the signaling molecule cAMP. GNAS functions downstream of several
CC       GPCRs, including beta-adrenergic receptors. Stimulates the Ras
CC       signaling pathway via RAPGEF2. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63092}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterotrimeric G proteins are composed of 3 units; alpha,
CC       beta and gamma. The alpha chain contains the guanine nucleotide
CC       binding site (By similarity). Interacts with CRY1; the interaction
CC       may block GPCR-mediated regulation of cAMP concentrations.
CC       Interacts with ADCY6 and stimulates its adenylyl cyclase activity
CC       (By similarity). Interacts with ADCY2 and ADCY5 (By similarity).
CC       Stimulates the ADCY5 adenylyl cyclase activity (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P04896, ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P63092}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8227063};
CC       Lipid-anchor {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8227063}.
