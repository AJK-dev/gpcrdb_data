CC   -!- FUNCTION: Guanine nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins) are
CC       involved as modulators or transducers in various transmembrane
CC       signaling systems. Transducin is an amplifier and one of the
CC       transducers of a visual impulse that performs the coupling between
CC       rhodopsin and cGMP-phosphodiesterase.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: G proteins are composed of 3 units; alpha, beta and
CC       gamma. The alpha chain contains the guanine nucleotide binding
CC       site. Interacts (when myristoylated) with UNC119; interaction is
CC       required for localization in sensory neurons.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21642972}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Rod. Predominantly expressed in the retina
CC       followed by the ciliary body, iris and retinal pigment epithelium.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22190596}.
CC   -!- DEVELOPMENTAL STAGE: Expressed at approximately postnatal day 7.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22190596}.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Night blindness, congenital stationary, autosomal
CC       dominant 3 (CSNBAD3) [MIM:610444]: A non-progressive retinal
CC       disorder characterized by impaired night vision, often associated
CC       with nystagmus and myopia. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17584859,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8673138}. Note=The disease is caused by
CC       mutations affecting the gene represented in this entry.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Night blindness, congenital stationary, 1G (CSNB1G)
CC       [MIM:616389]: An autosomal recessive form of congenital stationary
CC       night blindness, a non-progressive retinal disorder characterized
CC       by impaired night vision or in dim light, with good vision only on
CC       bright days. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22190596}. Note=The disease is
CC       caused by mutations affecting the gene represented in this entry.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the G-alpha family. G(i/o/t/z) subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
