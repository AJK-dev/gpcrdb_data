CC   -!- FUNCTION: Guanine nucleotide-binding proteins (G proteins)
CC       function as transducers downstream of G protein-coupled receptors
CC       (GPCRs) in numerous signaling cascades. The alpha chain contains
CC       the guanine nucleotide binding site and alternates between an
CC       active, GTP-bound state and an inactive, GDP-bound state.
CC       Signaling by an activated GPCR promotes GDP release and GTP
CC       binding. The alpha subunit has a low GTPase activity that converts
CC       bound GTP to GDP, thereby terminating the signal. Both GDP release
CC       and GTP hydrolysis are modulated by numerous regulatory proteins
CC       (PubMed:8774883, PubMed:18434541). Signaling is mediated via
CC       effector proteins, such as adenylate cyclase. Inhibits adenylate
CC       cyclase activity, leading to decreased intracellular cAMP levels
CC       (By similarity). The inactive GDP-bound form prevents the
CC       association of RGS14 with centrosomes and is required for the
CC       translocation of RGS14 from the cytoplasm to the plasma membrane.
CC       Required for normal cytokinesis during mitosis (PubMed:17635935).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10824, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17635935,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18434541, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8774883}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterotrimeric G proteins are composed of 3 units; alpha,
CC       beta and gamma. The alpha chain contains the guanine nucleotide
CC       binding site. Identified in complex with the beta subunit GNB1 and
CC       the gamma subunit GNG1 (PubMed:22383884). Identified in complex
CC       with the beta subunit GNB1 and the gamma subunit GNG2
CC       (PubMed:18434541). GTP binding causes dissociation of the
CC       heterotrimer, liberating the individual subunits so that they can
CC       interact with downstream effector proteins (PubMed:22383884).
CC       Interacts (GDP-bound form) with GPSM1; this inhibits guanine
CC       nucleotide exchange and GTP binding (By similarity). Interacts
CC       (GDP-bound form) with GPSM2 (via GoLoco domains); this inhibits
CC       guanine nucleotide exchange (PubMed:22952234). Interacts with
CC       RGS10; this strongly enhances GTP hydrolysis (PubMed:8774883,
CC       PubMed:18434541). Interacts with RGS1 and RGS16; this strongly
CC       enhances GTPase activity (PubMed:18434541). Interacts with RGS4
CC       (PubMed:18434541). Interacts with RGS12 (PubMed:18434541).
CC       Interacts (via active GTP- or inactive GDP-bound forms) with RGS14
CC       (via RGS and GoLoco domains) (PubMed:11976690, PubMed:18434541,
CC       PubMed:21115486, PubMed:22383884). Interacts with RGS3, RGS6,
CC       RGS7, RGS8, RGS17, RGS18 and RGS20 (in vitro) (PubMed:18434541).
CC       Interacts (GDP-bound form) with RIC8A (via C-terminus) (By
CC       similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P10824,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11976690, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16004878,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17264214, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18434541,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21115486, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22383884,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22952234, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8774883}.
